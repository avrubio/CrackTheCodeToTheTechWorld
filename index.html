Step 2: Network

Speak to like-minded individuals and ask an expert about the role that interests you!

So now that you have narrowed down the role you are interested in, I suggest you start looking for people to network with on LinkedIn and ask if they would have a coffee chat with you to learn more about their role, their day-to-day at the company they currently work at, and how they landed the role! Many people are willing to chat with others interested in switching to the tech field and will provide a lot of valuable advice that can help guide you on the next steps.

  • Set up a calendly to better organize your meetings
  • Have a list of open-ended questions you think will need to understand their role
  • ask them what job boards they used to find their role
  • what local communties they recommended joining

Step 4: optimize your resume

You may be thinking that none of your past roles or your current roles are relevant to the new tech role you want but that’s not the case! You have MANY transferrable skills that are valuable to you succeeding in your new tech role.

My favorite tool to use is ChatGPT to help me figure out ways to rewrite job duties! You will have to play around with your wording when asking ChatGPT to rewrite your transferable skills but my favorite way to start a prompt is by telling it to act as an expert in the subject area I am trying to fix, for example:

My prompt: I want you to act as an expert tech industry resume writer and reword this job duty in a way that shows my transferrable skills. “Handling and resolving customer complaints regarding product sales to customer service problems” ChatGPT response: Certainly! Here's a revised version that emphasizes your transferrable tech skills: "Managed and resolved customer inquiries spanning product sales and customer service, leveraging technical expertise to address issues seamlessly from initial contact through resolution. Demonstrated proficiency in troubleshooting and effectively communicating solutions, contributing to enhanced customer satisfaction and retention."

This is of course a little too long of a job duty, you always want to aim for one sentence per job duty but it’s a great baseline to nitpick the verbiage you’d like to use in your new resume template!

Don’t downsize any previous job you’ve had, they are all valuable life experiences that form your professional self to this day and have helped you reach to the point you are now! REMEMBER THAT!

Step 5: optimize your Linkedin and get noticed!

Your LinkedIn is one of the most important things employers see alongside your resume, especially in the tech industry. It’s also necessary because you will be using LinkedIn to network with people as I mentioned in Step 2.

Here’s a checklist of things you should be doing to optimize your LinkedIn:

  1. Get a professional headshot and use this on LinkedIn.
  2. Create an authentic banner that includes your name and portfolio, be creative with it don’t pick a stock photo on Canva and call it a day.
  3. Create a capturing headline but avoid using the words aspiring or looking for a tech role. Think of your headline as a short sweet elevator pitch. What do you bring to the table? I transform ideas into code OR I help companies automate their workflow. You could also ask ChatGPT for help with this with a similar prompt from the resume writing: I want you to act as an expert LinkedIn career coach, and help me write a good eye captivating headline, I am just starting out in tech and I am learning technologies to be _XYZ_.
  4. Write your summary and tell your reader why your switching to teach, what motivates you, and what you hope to accomplish if and when you land such a position!
  5. Once you know what role you’re aiming after, look at groups of people on LinkedIn who are working their way towards the same goal. As a Latina, I follow groups like Latina in Tech, Somos Tech, Coffee & Code, etc. A lot of the groups do meet-ups and you get to meet other like-minded individuals that are just starting out just like you or people already in the role you want!
  6. Document your journey! Write a post a day on LinkedIn and youse hashtags so you can get your posts noticed and interact with other people’s posts. As a developer I’ve personally done #100daysOfCode, which involves me dedicating an hour a day to coding and writing on LinkedIn what I learned each day. That got me noticed by quite a few people and helped me network with others. Remember when I said companies also look at your LinkedIn? Yeah well if you're documenting your journey you're showing evidence that you are dedicated to learning and becoming better at the role you are going after!
